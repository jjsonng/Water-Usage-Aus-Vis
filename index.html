Water Usage in Australia

Exploring Water Usage in Australia (2014-2022)

Where is all the water stored?

To investigate this, we can visualize all the water stations across Australia, with a particular focus on the clusters of stations within each state.

Currently, there are approximately 7700 water stations, most of which are situated in the South-East region of the country.

This distribution is expected, as having multiple water stations near the coast, where population density is higher, ensures easier access to water.

How has water been distributed throughout the years?

This bar chart illustrates the distributed water use by selected industries and households in Australia from 2014-15 to 2021-22. The industries represented include agriculture, forestry and fishing, households, manufacturing, mining, and other industries, with each category color-coded for clarity.

Agriculture, forestry, and fishing, shown in light blue, consistently consume the most water across all years.

The chart highlights significant variations in total water usage, peaking in 2017-18 and reaching its lowest point in 2020-21, as marked by the red dashed line.

Exploring Water Usage on Agriculture

Water Usage Breakdown on Farms

Overall, most of the water used on farms in Australia is allocated to “Cereals for grain or seed, excluding rice.” However, in Queensland, a significant portion of water is directed towards “Sugar cane.”

Similarly, in Tasmania and the Northern Territory, water usage is focused on other crops such as fruits, vegetables, and nuts, rather than grain or seed.

Total Water Used & Extracted on Farms

Are we using water efficiently across different states and territories?

However, are we using a considerable amount of water on farms in comparison to the amount we extract?

From a quick glance, we can see that Tasmania has the highest water consumption per capita, followed by New South Wales and Queensland

This is quite interesting, as these states have varying levels of industrial and agricultural activities, which significantly influence their water usage patterns. Tasmania, for instance, has high industry water consumption, contributing to its elevated per capita usage

Use

The relative amount of water use in comparison to the supply is appropriate, i.e., more supply than use.

In terms of what most of the states are using their water on, this is shown with electricity, gas, water, and waste services which households is a sub-category of.

Supply

The amount of water supply within Australia can mainly be attributed to Tasmania from their electricity, gas, water, and waste services.

This is quite surprising considering the land size of Tasmania in compairson to the other states within Australia, however this could also be due to the fact that most of the states were not recording values as much or accurately as Tasmania was.

How does Australia compare to the World?

World's Total Water Withdrawal

The amount of water withdrawal varies significantly across the world. The visualisation above shows the average level of water withdrawal during the latest recorded year (2017).

As defined in the AQUASTAT Database, water withdrawal is defined as the quantity of freshwater taken from groundwater or surface water sources (i.e., lakes or rivers) for use in services (houses or domestic), agricultural and industrial.

As shown, India has withdrawn the most water within the year 2017 by a considerable amount. In terms of where Australia lies at, the amount of water we have extracted is greater than 50% of the other countries.
